title: What Maps Leave Out
year: 2022
desc: On absence, cartographic silence, and the places that resist being named.
---

Every map is an argument about what matters. The cartographer decides: this river, yes; this path, no. This city, named; this settlement, implied by the road that ends near it. The blank spaces on historical maps are not empty — they are full of everything the mapmaker chose not to see, or could not, or was not permitted to.

What interests me is the negative space: the coastlines that trail off, the inland territories marked simply "unknown," the areas described only by what surrounds them. A place defined entirely by adjacency is still a place.
