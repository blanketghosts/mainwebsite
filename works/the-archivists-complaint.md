title: The Archivist's Complaint
year: 2024
desc: A cataloguer discovers that every object she indexes begins to forget its own history.
---

She begins each day with a fresh index card and the same problem: the objects do not cooperate. The tea tin she catalogued last Tuesday as "tin, decorative, floral motif, circa 1940s" has by Thursday become simply a tin. By the following Monday it is a container. By the end of the month it will be a thing that holds.

She has tried explaining this to her supervisor. He nods sympathetically and suggests she might benefit from more precise language. She nods back and does not say: I am using the most precise language available. The objects are forgetting themselves. I am only recording what they leave behind.
