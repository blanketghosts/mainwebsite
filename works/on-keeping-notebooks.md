There is a notebook from 2019 that I cannot read without feeling that I am trespassing. Not because the contents are private — they are, mostly, observations about weather and transit and the faces of strangers — but because the person who wrote them understood something about a Tuesday afternoon in March that I have since lost and cannot recover.

This is the thing about notebooks that no one mentions: they are not records of your thoughts. They are evidence that you had thoughts, once, that have since gone somewhere else.
